Job Satisfaction as Moderator Between Job Crafting and Employee Well-Being Among Employees in Malaysia

Introduction & Problem Statement

Background

  • Modern workplaces demand adaptability—prompting employees to engage in job crafting (proactively reshaping their roles) [1, 2].
  • Job crafting can enhance engagement and reduce stressors by proactively modifying job demands and resources [1, 2].
  • However, crafting alone may not lead to well-being unless employees are satisfied with their jobs, as job satisfaction can amplify the positive effects of crafting [1, 3, 5].

Problem Statement

  • Despite growing interest in job crafting, empirical clarity is lacking on how job satisfaction shapes its impact on employee well-being [1, 3, 5].
  • Prior studies often treat job satisfaction as an outcome, not as a moderator, creating a gap in understanding its role in the job crafting-wellbeing relationship [1, 3, 5].
  • In Malaysia, high burnout (67% in 2024) and low global WLB ranking (59/60) reflect deep well-being concerns, highlighting the need for context-specific research.

Rationale

Understanding how job satisfaction moderates this relationship can help organizations enhance employee well-being more effectively, particularly in the Malaysian context.

Theoretical Foundations & Literature Review

Key Theories

Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) Model

Job crafting is a personal resource; job satisfaction may amplify its benefits by allowing employees to self-adjust demands and resources, reducing stressors and increasing motivational factors [4, 5].

DRAMMA Model & Psychological Needs

The DRAMMA model (Detachment, Relaxation, Autonomy, Mastery, Meaning, Affiliation) underpins well-being. Job crafting directly targets these needs (e.g., greater autonomy, fostering affiliation) [1, 6, 9].

Key Constructs

  • Job Crafting: Employee-initiated changes to task, relationship, or cognitive aspects of the job to better fit their needs and preferences [1, 2].
  • Job Satisfaction: Positive emotional response to one's role, which can enhance motivation and perceived meaningfulness of work [1, 3, 5].
  • Employee Well-Being: Flourishing, general health, reduced emotional exhaustion, and psychological functioning at work, influenced by work–nonwork facilitation and perceived boundary control [1, 3].

Research Gap

  • Prior studies often treat job satisfaction as an outcome, not as a moderator, limiting understanding of its active role [1, 3, 5].
  • Lack of Malaysian-focused studies on this triadic relationship in a developing economy context, despite prevalent well-being concerns.

Research Questions & Objectives

🧩 Research Questions:

  1. What is the relationship between job crafting and employee well-being?
  2. How does job satisfaction relate to employee well-being?
  3. Does job satisfaction moderate the relationship between job crafting and employee well-being?

🎯 Research Objectives:

  • To examine the impact of job crafting on employee well-being.
  • To determine the relationship between job satisfaction and employee well-being.
  • To investigate whether job satisfaction moderates the relationship between job crafting and well-being.

Conceptual Framework

Job Crafting
→
Employee Well-Being
↑
Job Satisfaction (Moderator)

Hypotheses:

  • H1: Job crafting positively influences employee well-being [1, 3, 4].
  • H2: Job satisfaction positively influences employee well-being [1, 3, 5].
  • H3: Job satisfaction moderates the relationship between job crafting and well-being, such that higher job satisfaction amplifies the positive effects of job crafting on well-being [1, 3, 5].

Methodology Overview

Design

Quantitative cross-sectional study

Participants

Employees from public and private sectors in Malaysia

Instruments

  • Job Crafting: Tims & Bakker's Job Crafting Scale
  • Job Satisfaction: Minnesota Satisfaction Questionnaire (MSQ)
  • Well-Being: WHO-5 or similar validated well-being scale

Analysis

  • Descriptive and inferential statistics
  • Moderation analysis using SEM (SmartPLS or AMOS) to examine the strength of the relationship.

Anticipated Findings & Discussion

Anticipated Results

  • Job crafting will be positively associated with employee well-being, confirming its role in enhancing flourishing and reducing emotional exhaustion [4].
  • Job satisfaction will strengthen this positive relationship, indicating that satisfied employees experience greater well-being benefits from crafting [1, 3, 5].
  • Higher satisfaction will lead to stronger well-being benefits from crafting, potentially by amplifying motivational pathways and buffering against resource depletion [9, 11].

Discussion Points

  • Validates the moderating role of job satisfaction, shifting focus from it as merely an outcome to a crucial contextual factor (theoretical contribution) [1, 3, 5].
  • Highlights employee agency (through crafting) and organizational support (fostering satisfaction) as key to enhancing well-being [11, 14].
  • Offers practical implications for HR: Promote both job crafting opportunities and job satisfaction drivers to create a virtuous cycle of well-being [9, 11].

Novelty & Contributions

Theoretical Contribution

Shifts focus from job satisfaction as an outcome to a buffering moderator, integrating JD-R and COR perspectives in a novel way [1, 3, 5].

Extends understanding of how job satisfaction amplifies the positive effects of job crafting on well-being [9, 11].

Empirical Contribution

Provides data-driven insight into how Malaysian employees manage stress and satisfaction through job crafting, addressing a gap in developing economy contexts.

Offers empirical evidence on the interplay of job crafting, job satisfaction, and well-being in a specific cultural setting.

Practical Contribution

Informs HR strategies and leadership practices to enhance both crafting autonomy and satisfaction, leading to more resilient workforces [1, 10].

Suggests tailored interventions that combine training with digital tools to reinforce crafting habits and monitor satisfaction [1].

Conclusion & Research Status

Conclusion

This research investigates how job satisfaction influences the relationship between job crafting and employee well-being—bridging theory and practice in the Malaysian workplace context. The model posits that job crafting improves employee well-being, and this effect is moderated by the level of job satisfaction, such that employees with higher job satisfaction experience stronger well-being benefits from job crafting [1, 3, 5].

Status Update

Data collection phase ongoing; proposal successfully defended; analysis tools prepared.

Looking Forward

Results will offer actionable insights for designing employee-centric HR policies and fostering resilient, satisfied workforces capable of thriving amid evolving work demands. Future research will explore cultural variations and longitudinal impacts [1].
